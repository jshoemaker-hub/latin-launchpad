# Latin Story Integration Plan

This plan adds beginner Latin stories as a recurring lesson layer: vocabulary first, story scene second, quiz third. The story source set comes from Discover Latin's beginner story index, including Roman daily-life stories, school scenes, travel, trade, and myth/history readings.

## Classroom Routine

1. Preview 5-10 target words from the current lesson.
2. Show one illustration and ask students what they notice before translating anything.
3. Read or paraphrase the matching Discover Latin story aloud.
4. Ask students to listen for the lesson words in the story.
5. Finish with the app quiz, then have students say one tiny Latin sentence from the scene.

## Grade Use

| Grade band | Best story use | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 3-4 | One-picture micro-stories | Keep the Latin cue to one sentence and let students identify people, places, animals, and actions. |
| 5-6 | Short adapted scenes | Add subject/object questions: Who acts? What receives the action? |
| 7-8 | Full beginner readings | Use longer passages, grammar noticing, culture discussion, and short written responses. |

## Illustration Style Guide

- Use warm children's textbook art, not cartoon chaos.
- Show 3-5 clear vocabulary objects in every image.
- Keep Roman clothing and architecture plausible, but do not let historical detail bury the target words.
- Avoid gore, frightening close-ups, modern objects, labels, speech bubbles, and text inside the image.
- Leave open space around the focal action so the image can work on small screens.

- School: Children's textbook illustration of a Roman classroom, teacher with students using wax tablets and styluses, bright focused mood, historically plausible clothing, no text.
