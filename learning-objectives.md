# Latin Learning Objectives by Grade

Scope and sequence for grades 3–8, grounded in classical Latin pedagogy as presented in D'Ooge's *Latin for Beginners* and Mueller's *Latin 101: Learning a Classical Language*. Objectives spiral: each grade reinforces the prior year and adds one or two new structural ideas.

## Design principles
- Lower elementary (3–5) focuses on recognition, pronunciation, and vocabulary — no formal grammar drills.
- Upper middle (6–8) introduces morphology (endings), then syntax (case use), then short authentic reading.
- Every grade includes a culture/derivatives strand so students see Latin in the world around them.
- Mastery is measured by what a student can do, not what they can recite.

---

## Grade 3 — Latin is Real

**Theme:** Latin is a real language with familiar-feeling words.

By the end of grade 3, a student can:
- pronounce the Latin alphabet and the five long and short vowels using classical pronunciation
- read aloud about 40 first- and second-declension nouns (people, home, nature) with correct stress
- match each studied Latin word to its English meaning and to a picture
- identify at least 15 English words that come from studied Latin roots (e.g., *aqua* → aquarium, *stella* → stellar)
- recognize that Latin words change their endings, without yet naming the cases

**Vocabulary target:** ~50 concrete nouns; 10 high-frequency verbs in dictionary form (*amo, voco, porto*).

---

## Grade 4 — Words in Action

**Theme:** Things and actions; singular vs. plural.

By the end of grade 4, a student can:
- distinguish singular and plural endings for 1st-declension (*-a / -ae*) and 2nd-declension (*-us / -i*, *-um / -a*) nouns in the nominative
- conjugate the present tense of one 1st-conjugation verb (*amo, amas, amat, amamus, amatis, amant*) and recognize the pattern in others
- read and translate 2–3 word sentences of the form *puella cantat*, *pueri currunt*
- name the gender (masculine, feminine, neuter) of about 30 studied nouns
- connect at least 25 English derivatives to their Latin roots

**Vocabulary target:** ~75 words cumulative, including ~20 1st-conjugation verbs.

---

## Grade 5 — Subjects and Objects

**Theme:** Who is doing what to whom.

By the end of grade 5, a student can:
- identify the subject (nominative) and direct object (accusative) in a Latin sentence
- decline a 1st-declension noun in the singular through nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, ablative
- conjugate the present tense of 1st- and 2nd-conjugation verbs (*amo, moneo*) and recognize the difference in stem vowel
- translate simple Latin sentences with subject + verb + object (*magister discipulum laudat*)
- recognize the irregular verb *sum* in the present (*sum, es, est, sumus, estis, sunt*) and use it in copulative sentences (*puella bona est*)
- explain how word order in Latin differs from English

**Vocabulary target:** ~110 words cumulative; first 25 adjectives (1st/2nd declension).

---

## Grade 6 — Endings Carry Meaning

**Theme:** Cases and tenses; reading short stories.

By the end of grade 6, a student can:
- fully decline 1st- and 2nd-declension nouns in both numbers and identify all five cases by ending
- explain the basic use of each case: nominative (subject), genitive (possession), dative (indirect object), accusative (direct object), ablative (with/by/from)
- conjugate *amo* and *moneo* in the present, imperfect, and future active indicative
- conjugate *sum* in the present, imperfect, and future
- make a 1st/2nd-declension adjective agree with its noun in gender, number, and case
- translate short narrative passages (3–5 sentences) about Roman daily life or simple myth
- identify ~40 English derivatives across vocabulary studied so far

**Vocabulary target:** ~175 words cumulative; introduce 3rd-conjugation verbs (*rego*) in present only.

---

## Grade 7 — Reading Real Latin

**Theme:** Tense system, third declension, full agreement.

By the end of grade 7, a student can:
- conjugate verbs of all four conjugations (*amo, moneo, rego, audio*) in the present, imperfect, future, and perfect active indicative
- decline 3rd-declension nouns (consonant stems and i-stems) and recognize their gender patterns
- use personal, possessive, and demonstrative pronouns (*ego, tu, is, ea, id; hic, ille*) in context
- translate the relative pronoun *qui, quae, quod* in simple relative clauses
- read adapted passages from sources like Aesop, the Vulgate, or simplified Caesar (~50 words)
- explain at least three differences between Roman culture and modern American life encountered in readings (war, family, religion, slavery)
- recognize the passive voice in the present system and translate it correctly

**Vocabulary target:** ~250 words cumulative; ~30 verbs across all four conjugations.

---

## Grade 8 — Bridge to Real Texts

**Theme:** Full indicative, intro to subordinate clauses, authentic passages.

By the end of grade 8, a student can:
- conjugate any regular verb in all six tenses of the indicative, active and passive
- decline nouns of all five declensions and adjectives of the 1st/2nd and 3rd declensions
- form and use the comparative and superlative of adjectives and adverbs
- recognize and translate participles (present active, perfect passive) in agreement
- recognize the subjunctive mood and translate *cum* clauses and purpose clauses with teacher support
- read an unaltered short passage from a classical author (Caesar, Vulgate, or inscription, ~75 words) with a glossary
- count and write Roman numerals through 1000 and use cardinal numbers 1–20 in Latin
- write an original 4–6 sentence Latin paragraph using studied vocabulary and grammar
- explain how Latin shaped at least one feature of modern English grammar or vocabulary, citing examples

**Vocabulary target:** ~350 words cumulative; full active and passive indicative system.

## Cross-grade strands

Every grade should also build:
- **Pronunciation and oral reading** — classical pronunciation throughout; recite a short passage aloud each year.
- **Derivatives and English vocabulary** — students keep a running list; this is the strongest motivator for non-classical-school families.
- **Roman culture** — one age-appropriate theme per grade (family in 3, school in 4, city in 5, military in 6, myth in 7, government and law in 8).
- **Study habits** — flashcard practice, spaced review, and self-assessment; lesson dashboard reflects these.

## Alignment notes
- The grade 6–8 progression mirrors the sequence in D'Ooge Lessons I–XLVII (nouns, verbs, pronouns, third declension) and Mueller Lectures 1–22 (pronunciation through participles), adapted to age.
- Vocabulary targets are conservative; classical school programs that meet daily can comfortably exceed them.
- These objectives map directly to the existing `GRADE_WORDS` structure in `word-banks.js` (1st-decl nouns, 2nd-decl nouns, 1st-conj verbs, 2nd-conj verbs) and can drive the dashboard's mastery model.
